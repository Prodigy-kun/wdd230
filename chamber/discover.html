Ibdadan is known as the last of many firsts,the first television station in Africa on the 31st of October 1959, the first stadium to be built in Africa Obafemi Awolowo stadium,the first teaching hospital in Nigeria,Amongst the firsts also, is the popular 24 storey sky scraper- the Cocoa House, and so many more. Ibadan has always been a frontier in flourishing economics and diversity, and ANYA has recognized it's potentials and is determined to make the best use of the resourses to promote and protect the interest of her members

Mapo Hall

Mapo Hall is the colonial style city hall, perched on the top of Mapo Hill, in Ibadan, Oyo State, Nigeria. History has it that the hall was designed and constructed by Engineer Robert Taffy Jones in 1925 and is cited on the famous Mapo hill, which is the center of the metropolitan city of Ibadan. This building has witnessed a lot of cultural and political events.

University of Ibadan

The University of Ibadan is a public research university in Ibadan, Nigeria. The university was founded in 1948 as University College Ibadan, one of many colleges within the University of London. It became an independent university in 1962 and is the oldest degree-awarding institution in Nigeria.

Cocoa House

Cocoa House, completed in 1965 at a height of 105 metres, was once the tallest building in Nigeria and the first skyscraper in West Africa. It is located in Dugbe, a major commercial area in Ibadan, Oyo State, Nigeria. It was built from proceeds of agricultural commodities (e.g., Cocoa, Rubber, Timber) of the then Western State of Nigeria.

NTA

The Nigerian Television Authority or NTA is a Nigerian government-owned and partly commercial broadcast station. Originally known as Nigerian Television (NTV), it was inaugurated in 1977 with a monopoly on national television broadcasting, after a takeover of regional television stations by military governmental authorities in 1976. After declining interest from the public in government-influenced programming,The NTA runs the largest television network in Nigeria with stations in several parts of the country. It is widely viewed as the "authentic voice" of the Nigerian government.

Palms Shopping mall

With a total of 21,000 m2 retail space, Palms Ibadan presents a high quality family-friendly environment for major branded outlets, food, fashion and entertainment. The Palms Ibadan opened in June 2014. Ibadan, the capital of Oyo State in the south west of the country, is Nigeria’s third largest city in terms of population and the largest by geographic spread. It is a prominent transit point between the coast and the north of the country. Its anchor tenants is Shoprite.
